Last weekend I went on a whale watch with a philosopher and a historian. At the stern we saw the waves emerged along the trail of the ship. 
The edges of the trail form a cone, as told by one of my physicist friend who also told me that we are able to calculate the shape.
I thought this to be calculated via some complicated fluid dynamics and gave up thinking about it, because I know nothing about fluid. 
But then by the side of a philosopher, I believed that to be a good time to show off.

Interestingly, we really do not need to use fluid dynamics and the answer is much simpler than one thinks. 
The core idea is that, the edges of the trail is none other than the wavefront! We know from 1-D wave equation that the wavefronts travel at a velocity $$v_\rho$$.
